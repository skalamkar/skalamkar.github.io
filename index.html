I am hosted by Prof. François Baccelli. My broad research area is Wireless Communications.

In modern world, almost everybody (including a new-born baby) has been in touch with smartphones. And almost everybody owning a smartphone--willingly or unwillingly--has heard about associated hot words "WiFi, 3G, 4G, and 5G." And many probably wonder why sometimes the wireless internet on their phones works extremely fast and sometimes extremly slow. I do also wonder the same, and to understand what factors impact the quality of wireless communication, I use and develop the mathematical techniques in:

  • Stochastic geometry and point process theory to understand the effect of spatial aspect of user traffic.
  • Queueing theory to understand the effect of temporal aspect of user traffic.

I was a Simons postdoctoral researcher with Prof. Francois Baccelli at the University of Texas at Austin, Texas, USA and a postdoctoral research associate with Prof. Martin Haenggi at the University of Notre Dame, USA. I received my PhD in electrical engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kanpur, India.
